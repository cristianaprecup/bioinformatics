The study of bioinformatics involves the integration of computers, software tools, and databases.
Its primary goal is to address biological questions using large datasets.

The sequence of nucleotides determines the genetic instructions for the development of organisms.

The central dogma of molecular biology describes the flow of genetic information.
Transcription is the process where DNA is copied into messenger RNA molecules.
Translation is the process where ribosomes synthesize proteins using the RNA template.
Mutations can occur during DNA replication leading to genetic diversity or disease.
Single nucleotide polymorphisms are the most common type of genetic variation in people.
Genome wide association studies help identify genes involved in human disease.
Phylogenetics is the study of evolutionary relationships among biological entities.
BLAST is a popular algorithm used for comparing primary biological sequence information.
Protein structure prediction is a computationally complex problem in bioinformatics.
The folding of a protein chain is determined by its amino acid sequence.
Markov chains are mathematical systems that undergo transitions from one state to another.
In a first order Markov chain the probability of a future state depends only on the current state.
Hidden Markov Models are widely used in gene prediction and sequence alignment.
Next generation sequencing technologies allow for rapid DNA sequencing at low cost.
Bioinformaticians use Python and Rust to write efficient code for data analysis.

Systems biology focuses on complex interactions within biological systems.
Metabolomics is the scientific study of chemical processes involving metabolites.
Proteomics is the large scale study of proteins and their structures and functions.
Transcriptomics examines the expression levels of RNA transcripts in a cell.
The human genome project was a landmark global scientific effort.
CRISPR is a genetic engineering tool that allows for precise editing of DNA.
Epigenetics involves genetic control by factors other than an individuals DNA sequence.
Computational biology overlaps significantly with bioinformatics and systems biology.
Algorithms are essential for processing the vast amounts of biological data generated.
ClustalW is a commonly used computer program for multiple sequence alignment.

Stop codons signal the termination of protein translation in the ribosome.
Open reading frames are defined as spans of DNA between a start and stop codon.
